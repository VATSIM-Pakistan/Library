# New Islamabad International Airport / OPIS

## General Information

New Islamabad International Airport (OPIS) is the second-busiest airport in Pakistan, located in the capital, Islamabad. It serves as a major hub for domestic and international traffic, particularly to the Middle East, Europe, and North America.

The airport features modern infrastructure and supports wide-body operations including B777 and A330 aircraft. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Departure Procedures

### General

- Start-up includes enroute clearance and SID
- Initial climb typically FL160
- Pushback clearance required separately

### Taxi

- Expect holding at **J**
- Runway crossings require clearance

### Takeoff

- Follow SID strictly
- Contact Approach after departure

### Transition Altitude

12000 ft

## Arrival Procedures

### General

- Expect STAR + vectors
- Preferred approach: **ILS 28L**

### Notes

- Maintain speed/altitude restrictions
- Expect sequencing during peak hours

### Transition Level

Typically FL140
